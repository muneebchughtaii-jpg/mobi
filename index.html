Regrets & Taunts 💭

I think about the moments I didn't cherish enough when they were happening. The laughter we shared in the car, the late-night talks that felt like they could solve the world, the way you'd light up when talking about things you loved. I wish I had paid more attention, been more present, appreciated the simple magic of just being around you. There are things I said that I wish I could take back, and things I didn't say that I wish I had. I was afraid, unsure, caught between wanting something desperately and being terrified of losing it. That fear made me do stupid things, say hurtful things, and miss out on chances to show you how much you meant to me. If I could go back, I'd be braver. I'd be kinder. I'd tell you every single day that you were worth it. But I can't go back, so today, on your birthday, I'm telling you now: I'm sorry for the ways I fell short. I'm sorry for not fighting harder. I'm sorry for letting doubt win. And I'm grateful—so incredibly grateful—that you existed in my life, even if it was just for a chapter. You deserve someone who won't hesitate, who won't be afraid, who will love you the way you deserve to be loved. I hope you find that, and I hope life gives you everything you ever wanted and more. You were my greatest "what if," and that's something I'll carry with me forever. 💙
